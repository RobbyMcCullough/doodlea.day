How to draw... a whoopee cushion sticker

Build one squashed cushion, point the nozzle, add puff lines and a goofy face, then finish it with bold red marker. Keep the joke visual and text-free so the shape does the work.

15 minEasy

Felt-tip marker mode

Let's doodle a whoopee cushion sticker

Treat this as one playful practice round: sketch the idea loosely, simplify the shapes, then commit with confident marker outlines and bright fills.

Grab your stuff

Materials

  • Drawing paperSmooth paper helps marker lines
  • Black and colored markersOutlines plus bright fills
  • White gel penTiny shine marks when needed

Use black for the outline, red for the cushion, gray for the shadow, and the gel pen only for small shine marks.

  1. 01

    Squash the cushion

    Draw a rounded flattened cushion body, then mark a light pinched center guide.

    Doodle tip: Make the cushion wider than it is tall. The squashed shape is what makes the doodle read quickly.

  2. 02

    Point the nozzle

    Add a short nozzle sticking from one side, then draw a few curved puff lines near the opening.

    Doodle tip: Keep the puff marks simple and text-free. The little cloud shape should say enough.

  3. 03

    Ink the prank shape

    Trace the cushion, nozzle, and puff marks with a thick black marker outline.

    Doodle tip: Ink the existing shapes only. The outline should make the cushion bolder, not change its pose.

  4. 04

    Add the goofy face

    Draw two wide eyes, a silly smiling mouth, and a small offset shadow under the cushion.

    Doodle tip: Let the eyes be a little uneven. A goofy sticker works better when it is not too polished.

  5. 05

    Fill the cushion red

    Color the cushion and nozzle red, deepen the mouth, and leave a few small highlight gaps.

    Doodle tip: Use short marker strokes that follow the cushion curve so the fill keeps a handmade texture.

  6. 06

    Land the prank finish

    Retrace the existing outlines, deepen the red fill, and sharpen the face, puff marks, shadow, and highlights already on the page.

    Doodle tip: Stop before adding extra symbols. The cushion, face, and puff marks are enough for the joke.
